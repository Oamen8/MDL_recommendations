I decided a long time ago that I should watch it, I am a big fan of Kim Jae Wook, and it looked exiting. But since I couldn't find the subtitles for it, I haven't watched it before now..

It turns out that you don't really need subtitles, after watching a few Korean dramas, you can perfectly understand what the topic of their conversations are(or at least I think so.. :P), and since it is only 10 minutes long, it's not that much talking.. :)

It was a really horrible story, but when you're thinking about the underlying topic of it, it's really cute.. It sounds weird, but its true.. It was a beautiful love story.. And the feelings are expressed very well in it too, so you(or at least I) get dragged in to it, and you are happy when they are happy, and sad when they are sad.
I also got a lot of empathy with the girl.. :)

I hope you watch it! It's definitely worth your time.. And Jae wook looks amazing as always!

Several unanswered questions

I just watched for the challenge thinking it is only 10 mins but those ten minutes felt like 100 minutes. In those 100 minutes, I had several questions like- Who is under the table? Why watch that tv show? How did she end up here? Do they eat that? etc. It was more of peeking into the life of the couple and nothing more. Was it enjoyable? Not per say. I liked how whistle was a nod to the classic movie Psycho. What did I take from it? Nothing. Was it boring? not at all. It was a very disturbing, yet, different experience. Made for the art.
